## Checklist for College Students  

A quick review could save you from losing your hard-earned money or your personal data.  

### Your 8-Point Safety Checklist - UPI and Financial Safety  


##### Remember the Golden UPI Rule  
You never have to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Your PIN is only used when you are sending money. If anyone asks you to “enter your PIN to receive money,” stop immediately and disconnect. This is always a scam.  
Never approve a UPI Collect Request from a stranger
If you receive a “Collect Request” or “Payment Request” on PhonePe, Google Pay, or any other UPI app from a person you do not know, reject it immediately. Approving this request will deduct money directly from your account.  


##### Account and Password Safety  

Use a strong and unique password for every app.  
Do not use your name, birthday, or simple numbers like “1234” as your password. Use a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols. Most importantly, do not use the same password for your email account, banking app, and social media accounts. This is because if your password is stolen for one app, it can compromise access to your other accounts.    
Turn on Two-Factor Authentication on all important apps.  
Two-Factor Authentication is also known as 2FA or OTP Login. This is an extra layer of security. This means that even if your password is stolen, no one can log in to your account because they need your permission for that.  


##### Safe Browsing and Link Safety  

Never click on the link sent by unknown SMS or WhatsApp messages.  
Scammers send fake messages that look exactly like messages sent by SBI, HDFC, PhonePe, or IRCTC. These messages may silently steal your login information. Always open the app by tapping on the icon, never by clicking on the link sent by others.  
Always verify the site before typing anything personal.  
Scammers may create fake websites that look exactly the same as the original ones. Always verify the site before typing in your password, card number, or OTP on a website. Ensure that the address bar starts with "https://" and the domain name is correctly spelled, for example, "sbi.co.in" instead of "sbi-secure-login.com."    


##### Scam Awareness and Smart Behaviour  

Be extremely cautious of too-good-to-be-true offers.  
Offers like free gifts, lottery wins, part-time jobs that pay thousands of rupees per hour, or scholarship refunds may be scams. Scammers may also create a fake urgency like "claim in the next 10 minutes!" to prevent you from thinking clearly. Always think twice before acting.  
Never share your OTP with others, not even the bank.   
No bank, PhonePe, Google Pay, or genuine companies will ever call you and ask for your OTP, CVV, or full card number. If anyone asks for this information, hang up immediately and call your bank's official helpline to report it.   


##### If You Are a Victim — Act Immediately

Call your bank right away to report the transaction and request a freeze.  
File a complaint within 24 hours for the best chance of getting your money back.  
Save screenshots of all chats, transaction IDs, and phone numbers as evidence.  


Report crimes at the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline at 1930.
